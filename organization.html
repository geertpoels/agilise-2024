Organizing Committee

Palash Bera, Saint Louis University, USA
Palash Bera is an endowed Father Davis Professor and the Department Chair of the ITM and Operations Department of Chaifetz School of Business, Saint Louis University. He has a Master’s and a Ph.D. degree in Management Information Systems from the University of British Columbia, Vancouver. Palash’s research and teaching interests are in Design Thinking, Data Visualization, Storytelling, and Predictive Analytics. He teaches undergraduate and graduate data analytics and design thinking courses. He has several research publications in the top information systems journals such as MIS Quarterly and Information Systems Research. He was co-organizer of Agil-ISE'22 and Agil-ISE'23.
Yves Wautelet, KULeuven, Belgium
Yves Wautelet is associate professor in Information Systems at KU Leuven (campus Brussel) as well as invited professor at Université catholique de Louvain and Université de Namur. He formerly has been an IT project manager and a postdoc fellow at the Université catholique de Louvain, Belgium. He completed a PhD thesis focusing on project and risk management issues in large enterprise software design. Yves also holds a bachelor and master in management sciences as well as a master in Information Systems. He acted as a program co- chair of RCIS 2019. His research interests include aspects of software engineering such as agile requirements engineering, model-driven software development, IT Governance, project management supporting development life cycles, CASE-tools as well as IT Governance and information systems strategy. He was co-organizer of Agil-ISE'22 and Agil-ISE'23.
Geert Poels, Ghent University, Belgium
Geert Poels is a Senior Full Professor and leads the Business Informatics research group at the Department of Business Informatics and Operations Management of the Faculty of Economics and Business Administration of Ghent University, Belgium. He coordinates the Enterprise Modeling, Architecture, and Engineering (EMEA) research cluster of the UGent Business Informatics research group. Geert was Program Chair of CAiSE'22 and therefore could not officially co-organize the Agil-ISE'22 workshop, while he was involved from the start in establishing the Agil-ISE workshop series. This year, Geert is proud of being a co-organizer of the Agil-ISE'23 workshop. He is supervisor of the PhD research of Abhimanyu Gupta (NLP-based automatic generation of conceptual models from BDD scenarios) and Anis Amna (Identifying and remedying ambiguity in user stories), who both presented their work at Agil-ISE'22.
